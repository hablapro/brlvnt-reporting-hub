# Monthly Report Template

## Purpose
This template defines the standard structure for a monthly marketing report.

Use it for:
- client reporting
- internal performance reviews
- monthly paid media summaries
- channel-specific or cross-channel performance updates

This report should be:
- concise
- insight-driven
- client-friendly
- action-oriented

Do not turn the report into a raw spreadsheet dump.

## 1. Executive Summary

### Purpose
Provide a high-level summary of the month.

### Include
- overall performance direction
- whether results were on track against goals
- major wins
- major risks
- high-level business impact

### Writing Rules
- keep to 1 to 2 short paragraphs or 3 to 5 bullets
- focus on insight, not metric overload
- prioritize what leadership or the client should know first

### Template
- Performance this month was [strong / mixed / below target] relative to [goal / prior month / benchmark].
- The most important positive driver was [campaign / audience / channel / creative / placement].
- The biggest challenge was [cost efficiency / volume / viewability / conversion quality / tracking issue].
- Based on current results, the account is [on pace / off pace / improving] toward [goal].

## 3. Campaign Analysis

### Purpose
Explain what happened and why.

### Include
- delivery trends
- efficiency changes
- conversion shifts
- audience performance
- placement or publisher quality
- creative or format impact
- pacing issues
- tracking or attribution notes

### Writing Rules
- separate facts from interpretation
- explain drivers, not just outcomes
- keep to 3 to 6 focused bullets or short paragraphs

### Template
- Spend [increased / decreased] by [x%], driven primarily by [cause].
- Conversion volume changed by [x%], largely due to [cause].
- Efficiency improved / declined because of [audience / creative / placement / bid / tracking / landing page factor].
- Quality indicators such as [viewability / frequency / bounce rate / impression share] suggest [interpretation].

***

## 4. Campaign Highlights

### Purpose
Call out standout wins and meaningful positive signals.

### Include
- best-performing campaigns
- strongest creatives
- highest-quality audiences
- top placements or publishers
- successful optimizations
- notable momentum shifts

### Template
- [Campaign name] was the top performer based on [metric].
- [Audience / placement / creative] delivered the strongest efficiency at [value].
- A recent optimization to [budget / targeting / creative / placement mix] improved [metric].

***

## 5. Challenges / Risks

### Purpose
Make issues visible before they become bigger problems.

### Include
- rising CPA / CPL
- declining conversion rate
- weak viewability
- overfrequency
- poor placement quality
- low impression share
- tracking gaps
- underdelivery or pacing problems

### Template
- [Issue] is creating risk for [goal].
- If this trend continues, it may affect [efficiency / scale / revenue / lead volume].
- The most likely cause is [cause], though [caveat] should also be monitored.

***

## 6. Suggested Optimizations

### Purpose
Translate the analysis into action.

### Include
- budget reallocation
- audience refinement
- creative rotation
- placement exclusions
- bid / pacing changes
- landing page tests
- tracking fixes
- channel mix adjustments

### Writing Rules
- make each recommendation specific
- tie it to observed performance
- avoid vague advice

### Template
- Reallocate budget from [low-performing area] to [high-performing area] to improve [metric].
- Reduce exposure in [weak placements / audiences] due to [reason].
- Test [new creative / offer / audience / landing page] to address [problem].
- Investigate and resolve [tracking or data issue] before the next reporting cycle.

***

## 7. Next Steps

### Purpose
Clarify what will happen next month.

### Include
- actions to be executed
- tests to be launched
- metrics to watch
- issues to validate
- priorities for the next cycle

### Writing Rules
- keep short and concrete
- prioritize execution

### Template
- Launch [test / optimization].
- Shift budget toward [campaign / audience / channel].
- Monitor [metric] closely due to [reason].
- Validate [tracking / attribution / naming / feed issue].
- Report back on [specific hypothesis or initiative].

***

## 9. Data Notes

### Always document
- attribution model
- source systems
- blended vs platform-reported logic
- missing data
- delayed conversions
- data quality caveats

### Template
- Conversion reporting is based on [source] using [attribution window].
- Revenue reflects [platform-reported / analytics-reported / CRM-reported] values.
- The following limitations affected this month’s analysis: [list].

***

## Monthly Summary Tab Version

When adapting this template for a dashboard Summary tab, use this order:
1. KPI Overview
2. Executive Summary
3. Campaign Analysis
4. Campaign Highlights
5. Suggested Optimizations
6. Next Steps

Keep the Summary tab short enough to scan quickly while still giving context and actionability.
